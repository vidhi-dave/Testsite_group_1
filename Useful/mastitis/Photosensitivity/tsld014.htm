New Zealand Syndromes

  • Groups of calves- more than 1 affected with peeling skin or sometimes ears shrivelling up.

  • Usually not associated with liver damage.

  • Theory is that the livers of recently weaned calves are not used to processing large amounts of phylloerythrin found in lush “spring” grass and it “overflows” into the bloodstream

  • -influence of ERD calf rearing -sudden access to grass? Sudden change in diet

  • -immature liver busy metabolising vaccines, anthelmintics etc
