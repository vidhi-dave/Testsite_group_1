Which Dry Cow treatment to choose - in practice



Like milking cow treatments there are a variety of dry cow therapy products on the market.
See the Table of Dry Cow Preparations

These belong either in the penicillin, semi-synthetic penicillin or cephalosporin categories or any of these combined with steptomycin.

These preparations often contain similar anti-microbial drugs to milking cow preparations but in slow release bases which ensure a persistence of the drug in the udder.

Dry cow treatments can also be categorised according to their persistence in the udder. Most "short acting" dry cow therapies claim that anti-microbial levels in the udder are above MIC's for common pathogens for about four to five weeks.
The "long acting" dry cow therapy preparations claim up-to ten weeks depending upon brand.

Again like milking cow treatments exactly which brands are stocked is usually the decision of the senior veterinarians in a practice. The choice is often made based upon the same reasons as for milking cow treatments however practices will stock both short and long acting brands.

Deciding which product to use on a particular farm is usually based upon:

  • Past experience of the farmer / vet.
  • Results of culture and sensitivity tests done during the season.
  • Length of action of the drug.
  • Cost to farmer.
  • Due calving dates

All products are similarly effective at preventing clinical mastitis in the three weeks after drying-off.

Some products are probably better suited than others to treat existing intra-mammary infections depending upon the bacteria involved and the MIC for that anti-microbial drug. However we cannot culture the milk of all cows so an informed choice must be made on the history of the farm or culture results as to which drugs are appropriate.
e.g. A penicillin product is not well suited for a farm with a history of a S.aureus problem based on the in-vitro sensitivity of this bacteria, however the product could be effective against streptococci.

There is evidence that the longer acting preparations are more effective at preventing the acquisition of infections later in the dry period which may become clinical mastitis in the peri-calving period.

Whereas most milking cow preparations are similarly priced, the price of dry cow treatments vary considerably according to the length of udder persistence. This is an important factor to consider especially if a large number of cows is to be treated.

It is important to consider the due calving dates when choosing dry cow therapy do not choose a product if the cow is due to calve within the withholding time or residues could be present in the milk. If a cow calves within the withholding time of a product she must be withheld from supply until the withholding time has elapsed or the milk can be demonstrated to be free of residues.

Remember Dry cow therapy treatments are prescription animal remedies which can only be supplied to bona-fide clients following a veterinary consultation. A protocol for the supply of dry cow therapy has been devised by the veterinary council of New Zealand.
