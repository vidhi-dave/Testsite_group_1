Definition

Mastitis

  • Inflammation of the mammary gland, almost always caused by infecting microorganisms, primarily bacteria.

 

Clinical Mastitis

  • Udder inflammation characterised by visible abnormalities in the milk, and/or udder, and/or systemically.
  • Milk changes: clots, flakes, colour variation, abnormal odour and texture.
  • Udder changes: swelling, heat, pain, redness, fever.

 

Subclinical Mastitis

  • Inflammation of the mammary gland that is found only detected using a diagnostic test. Somatic cell count is the most used diagnostic test.
  • Subclinical mastitis is the most prevalent form of the disease.

 

 

 

Mastitis is one of the most prevalent economically important production diseases affecting dairy cattle world-wide.
