Overview of cardiovascular system pharmacology

The links are to the vet students' study guide, which contains far more information than you need to know. Use for background reading only!!!

cardiac arrest

 treatment

cardiopulmonary resuscitation
± adrenaline

 Nurses must know how to carry out CPR and give adrenaline.

acute heart failure

 possible treatment

oxygen

inotropes

  • adrenaline
  • dobutamine
  • dopamine

sodium bicarbonate

anti-arrhythmics

  • lignocaine
  • verapamil
Nurses should know which drugs are likely to be used and how they are normally given so that the drugs can be given as quickly as possible.

congestive heart failure

 possible treatment

angiotensin converting enzyme inhibitors

  • benazepril
  • enalapril
  • quinapril, etc

diuretics

  • frusemide

inotropes

  • digoxin
  • phosphodiesterase inhibiitors
    • theophylline derivatives
    • milrinone
    • pimobendan
Nurses should know how to advise owners about minor side effects and how to cope with major side effects until a veterinary assessment can be made.
