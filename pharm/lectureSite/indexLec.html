Old lectures

These lectures are the ones given in the old curriculum which contain more detailed info than you are expected to remember for the new curriculum!

Most of these lectures are in pdf format, which requires the free Adobe Acrobat to view. They are designed to be looked at, not printed. They can be printed at up to 16 slides per page. If you really must waste trees by printing stuff out, the outlines are much smaller and do not have the backgrounds or photos. Remember that printing is not a substitute for learning! There are lots of free pdf annotation apps out there if you want to add to the slides.

The animations may not work in all web browsers: if you have problems, use the study guide web pages.

If you can't resist my dulcet tones, some of the lectures are available as audio or enhanced podcasts. The m4a files are large (10 - 20MB) and contain voice and slides. The mp3 files only contain audio and are slightly smaller. Massey does not support this sort of thing, and my hardware is very old, so unfortunately there are gaps.

The cases are the basic info on the case used in that lecture. They are included so you can think about them in advance, or even (if you're really keen), research all the possible treatment options and why some are not as good as others!
