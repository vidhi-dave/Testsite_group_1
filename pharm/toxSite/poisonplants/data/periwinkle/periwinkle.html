periwinkle

Vinca major

Other names

Blue Periwinkle, Vinca, Sorcerer's violet, Big leaf periwinkle, Greater periwinkle, Large Periwinkle, Blue buttons

Description

An evergreen trailing vine that forms a dense mat across the ground. Leaves are dark green and glossy. The hairless stems spread by putting down roots from the nodes present on stems. Flowers are purple-blue and tubular, up to 5cm across.

Similar plants

Lesser/Common Periwinkle (Vinca minor)

Distribution

Throughout NZ, especially under hedges and waste areas (Nelson)

Toxin

Contains cardiac glycosides that are especially concentrated in the leaves. Cardiac glycosides inhibit sodium-potassium pumps in the heart, decreasing electrical conductivity through the heart.

Species affected

Livestock, cats, dogs

Clinical signs acute

Rapid breathing; cold extremities; gastrointestinal disturbances including abdominal pain and diarrhoea

Clinical signs chronic

Hallucinations; rapid, weak, and irregular pulse; heart arrhythmias; conduction disturbances resulting in decreased cardiac output and death

Post mortem signs

Haemorrhage, congestion, oedema, and cell degeneration throughout tissues; multifocal, myocardial degeneration and necrosis in less acute poisonings; evidence of plant ingestion

Diagnosis

History

Differential diagnosis

Treatment

Gastric lavage, induce vomiting, activated charcoal. Treat arrhythmias if necessary

Prognosis

Good unless a large quantity has been ingested (unlikely)

Prevention

Removing plants from environment
