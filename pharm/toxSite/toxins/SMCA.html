Chlorinated Aliphatic Acids

This group of weedkillers includes sodium mono chloroacetate (SMCA) and sodium trichloroacetate (STCA) trichlorobenzoic acid (2,3,6 TBA), DICAMBA and DALAPON 2,2 dichloropropionic acid. The sprays are absorbed by the roots and leaves, translocated and produce a knockdown and residual effect.

Toxicity

These are relatively harmless herbicides if used correctly. Losses have however been recorded in sheep and cattle.

Clinical signs

Stiffness, unsteady gait, recumbency and extensor paralysis of the limbs have been seen in sheep and cattle. Dalapon has produced abortion and the birth of weakly lambs with a mortality rate of 80%

Post mortem

Severe widespread haemorrhages were seen in the carcasses at autopsy with marked oedema and severe venous congestion of the neck and thorax. .
