Information on doses for drugs licensed for use in animals should be obtained from the IVS or the manufacturer’s data sheet. The information here is intended as a guide only. Remember, unlicensed drugs are given on your responsibility. Remember withholding times in food animals too. The list of drugs available in NZ (and their price!) is subject to change. Errors and omissions, email me.

pancuronium

competitive muscle relaxant

indications

muscle relaxation under anaesthesia

side effects

apnoea - give IPPV, tachycardia

precautions

animal must be unconscious

dose

dog & horse 60µg/kg iv lasts about 45min

paroxetine

SSRI antidepressant

indications

behaviour problems

side effects

anorexia, anxiety

dose

dog and cat 2.5 - 5mg/animal once daily po

pentazocine

opioid partial agonist (k agonist?)

indications

moderate pain, anaesthetic premed

side effects

bizarre behaviour - hallucinations?

dose

cats 2 - 3 mg/kg sc, im, iv

note

little used

pentobarbitone

pentobarbital USAN

barbiturate anaesthetic, controlled drug C4

indications

euthanasia, long term anaesthesia

dose

euthanasia - 120 - 200mg/kg iv

anaesthesia - 15 - 25mg/kg iv

notes

beware splashes in eyes etc - it will anaesthetise people too.

pentosan polysulphate

anti-arthritis drug

indications

adjunct therapy to correct the cause of osteoarthritis (cruciate or intra articular fracture), chronic osteoarthritis, primary osteoarthritis, degenerative joint disease

contra-indications

infection, clotting defects or traumatic haemorrhage, liver or kidney disease

side effects

local reaction post-injection when given intra-articularly. Heparin like clotting problems and immune mediated hypersensitivity have occurred in people.

precautions

aseptic injection technique compulsory

dose

Horse 120-240mg intra-articularly every 7 days for 3-4 treatments or im or sc 500mg every 4 days for up to 7 treatments (probably not as effective)

Dog 10mg/kg orally or 3mg/kg im every 7 days minimum or as needed

note

inhibits new blood vessel growth - being investigated for tumours and chronic inflammation

pethidine

meperidine USAN

weak µ opioid agonist - controlled drug B3

indications

moderate pain, colic, anaesthetic premed

side effects

respiratory depression with anaesthetics

dose

cats & dogs 3.5 - 10mg/kg im, 10 - 15mg/kg sc every 2 - 3 hours at least

horses 1 - 2mg/kg im every 1 - 2 hours

pig & sheep 2mg/kg im or iv every 2 - 3 hours

note

too short acting to be much use in most circumstances

phenobarbitone

phenobarbital USAN

anticonvulsant - controlled drug C5

indications

status epilepticus, prevention of seizures

side effects

sedation, polyphagia, increased liver enzymes

dose

prevention of seizures dogs & cats 1.5 - 5mg/kg po twice daily

horses 4 - 10mg/kg po twice daily

status epilepticus (use a benzodiazepine first) dogs and cats 3 - 6mg/kg iv to effect every 6 - 12 hours

foals 20mg/kg slow iv over 30 mins

plasma levels

dog - 15 - 45 µg/ml (65 - 194 µM) but usually in the range 25 - 35 µg/ml (108 - 150 µM)

with bromide - 9 - 36 µg/ml

cat - 10 - 30 µg/ml (50 - 140 µM)

note

1µM = 0.232µg/mL

1µg/mL = 4.3µM

phenoxybenzamine

adrenergic α antagonist

indications

relaxation of internal sphincter of bladder in urinary retention (often given with diazepam)

contraindications

heart disease

dose

dogs - 0.25 - 0.5mg/kg po 2 - 3 times daily

cats - 0.5mg/kg po twice daily

note

binds irreversibly to receptors so plasma levels give no indication of clinical effect.

phenylbutazone

NSAID

indications

inflammation, pain

side effects

gut ulceration, kidney failure, blood dyscrasias

dose

cattle - 2 - 5mg/kg iv, 4 - 8mg/kg po every other day not recommended - very long withholding period

pigs - 2 - 4mg/kg iv or po daily

horses - withholding period 6 months

phenylpropanolamine

adrenergic α1 agonist

indications

increase bladder internal spnincter tone (usually hormonal incontinence)

precautions

cardiovascular disease

dose

dog 1.5mg/kg po every 8 - 12 hrs, can be inceased to 3mg/kg if side effects allow, then reduce to minimum that works

phenytoin

anticonvulsant / class 1 antiarrhythmic

indications

digitalis toxicity

contraindications

cats, liver disease, pregnancy

pharmacokinetics

metabolised in the liver, very short half life in dogs compared to humans, dose dependent (zero order) elimination kinetics in humans, not in dogs or the horse

dose

antiarrhythmic dog 35-50 mg/kg po three times daily

anticonvulsant dog 10 - 35mg/kg po three times daily and adjust as necessary

plasma levels

10 - 20µg/mL (40 - 80µM)

phytomenadione

vitamin K1

phytonadione USAN, phylloquinone to chemists

indications

warfarin type anticoagulant overdose

side effects

occasional anaphyllactoid reactions after iv injection (vehicle)

dose

dog 2.5 - 5 mg/kg iv / sc (at several sites) depending on urgency of situation; then po:

warfarin 1mg/kg for 6 days

brodifacoum 2.5mg/kg for 3 weeks

dipacinone 5mg/kg for 4 weeks

similar protocols have been used in cats, but there is no info on their safety

pilocarpine

muscarinic agonist

indications

glaucoma

contra-indications

anterior uveitis, anterior lens luxation

side effects

local irritation

dose

several drops of 1 or 2% solution on cornea 3 - 4 times daily

pralidoxime

cholinesterase reactivator

indications

organophosphate poisoning, carbamate poisoning?

care

myaesthenia gravis

dose

horses and cattle 10 - 40mg/kg slowly iv

dogs 20 - 50mg/kg slowly iv

cats - 20mg/kg slowly iv

note

give atropine 25 - 200µg/kg as well

expensive

prazosin

α1 adrenoreceptor antagonist

indications

obsolescent vasodilator - adjunctive therapy of congestive heart failure particularly secondary to mitral or aortic valve insufficiency when hydralazine is not effective or not tolerated, treatment of systemic hypertension or pulmonary hypertension in the dog, or in dogs that do not respond well to other agents.

side effects

hypotension, may get syncope (orthostatic hypotension), CNS signs, GIT signs, tolerance develops

care

chronic renal failure

dose

Dog 0.5 - 2mg/animal po 2 - 3 times daily - titrate to effect and monitor

procainamide

class 1 antiarrhythmic

indications

ventricular tachyarrhythmias (supraventricular arrhythmias in high doses?)

pharmacokinetics

Absorption po - sustained release tablets usually used in dogs

Metabolism short tl/2 of 2.5 - 4.7 hours therefore require either an iv infusion or sustained release. unlike humans not extensively metabolised with 50-70% of an iv dose excreted unchanged

Elimination 90% of an iv dose excreted in urine within 24 hours

side effects

QRS duration and Q-T interval prolongation, can also stimulate ventricular tachyarrhythmias. Higher concentrations decrease myocardial contractility and produce hypotension; Gut disturbances

care

renal disease - reduce dose

dose

Dogs 8 - 30 mg/kg po 4 times daily (ordinary tablets) or 3 times daily (sustained release tablets)

note

Now only available as injection.

prochlorperazine

phenothiazine antiemetic

indications

intractable vomiting

side effects

sedation, hypotension

dose

dogs and cats 100 - 200µg/kg 3 - 4 times daily im, or 50µg/kg 3 - 4 times daily iv

propofol

injectable anaesthetic

dose

dogs - unpremedicated 5 - 6mg/kg iv, premedicated 3- 4mg/kg iv, alfentanil premed 1.5 - 2mg/kg

cats unpremedicated 8mg/kg iv, premed 6mg/kg iv

note

some cats have a slow recovery

propranolol

non specific ß blocker

indications

tachyarrhythmias, hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, hyperthyroid-ism

side effects

bronchospasm, myocardial depression, bradycardia, hypotension

contraindications

overt heart failure, greater than first degree heart block, asthma or chronic lower airway disease due to beta blocking as it may further constrict airways

care

diabetics - decreased sympathetic compensation for hypo-glycaemia, renal or hepatic insufficiency, in combination with digitalis

pharmacokinetics

Absorption biavailability between 2 - 17% after first pass effect in the liver

Distribution highly lipid soluble and readily crosses the blood brain barrier

Metabolism has extensive first pass effect in the liver, half life in dog is 1-2 hours, effects seen longer than half life because of active metabolites and receptor binding

Elimination renal with less than 1% unchanged

dose

ventricular arrhythmias dogs 20 - 60 µg/kg iv slowly then 100µg/kg po three times daily increasing over a week to a max of 1mg/kg three times daily

cats 40µg/kg slowly iv then 2.5mg/kg po three times daily increasing to a max of 10mg/kg three times daily

protamine

indications

heparin overdose

side effects

transient hypotension

dose

1mg for every 100units heparin slowly iv

pseudoephedrine

adrenergic α1 agonist

indications

urinary incontinence in bitches

nasal decongestion in cats

side effects

restlessness, sleeplessness

Does not cross blood brain barrier to any great extent

availability

human nasal decongestants / cold remedies

dose

dog 15 - 60mg po 2 - 3 times daily

cat 2 - 4mg/kg twice daily
