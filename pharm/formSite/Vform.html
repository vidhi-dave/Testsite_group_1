Information on doses for drugs licensed for use in animals should be obtained from the IVS or the manufacturer’s data sheet. The information here is intended as a guide only. Remember, unlicensed drugs are given on your responsibility. Remember withholding times in food animals too. The list of drugs available in NZ (and their price!) is subject to change. vecuronium

competitive muscle relaxant

indications

muscle relaxation under anaesthesia

contra-indications

liver disease, no facilities for IPPV

side effects

apnoea - give IPPV

precautions

animal must be unconscious

dose

dog, horse & cats 100µg/kg iv (lasts 15 - 20min) then increments of 20µg/kg iv

vedaprofen

NSAID

indications

arthritis, soft tissue injuries, etc

side effects

gastric ulceration, kidney failure

pharmacokinetics

half life dog 10 - 12 h (man 2 - 3 h)

dose

dog 0.5 - 1mg/kg po

verapamil

calcium channel blocker

indications

severe acute supraventricular tachyarrhythmias, atrial flutter or fibrillation.

pharmacokinetics

Absorption > 90%, food will decrease the rate and extent of absorption

Distribution in humans is 90% protein bound -Vd is about 4.5 1/kg in dogs, it crosses the plasma and the placenta in humans

Metabolism extensive metabolism in the liver with a first pass effect gives bioavailability of 10 - 23% though if there is hepatic dysfunction then this may increase, tl/2 is 0.8 - 2.5 hours in the dog

Elimination most of the metabolites excreted via the urine

side effects

peripheral vasodilation - hypotension, decreases myocardial contractility, bradycardia

contraindications

severe congestive heart failure, hypotension, sick sinus syndrome, 2nd or 3rd AV block, digitalis intoxication, do not use with propanolol

dose

dog - 0.05 - 0.15 mg/kg slowly iv over 5 minutes then infusion of 2 -10 µg/kg/min or repeat slow injections at 30 minutes intervals

oral- if impaired myocardial function then 0.1 - 0.2 mg/kg twice daily initially and titrate to effect:- if normal myocardium then can dose up to 5 - 10 mg/kg three times daily

vinblastine

anticancer drug

indications

rescue of lymphoma

side effects

myelosuppression, nausea and vomiting. Irritant perivascularly

precautions

care in liver disease - reduce dose

dose

dog & cat 2.5mg/m2 once weekly

note

relatively cheap

vincristine

anticancer drug

indications

lymphoma and some solid tumours such as sarcomas in combination with doxorubicin and cyclophosphamide

side effects

highly irritant if injected perivascularly, peripheral neuropathy, pancreatitis, constipation and ileus

dose

dog & cat 0.5-0.75mg/m2 iv or 0.025 mg/kg iv (cat) Usually given every week for first 6-8 weeks then every 2-3 weeks thereafter

note

Light and temperature sensitive so store in a refrigerator.
