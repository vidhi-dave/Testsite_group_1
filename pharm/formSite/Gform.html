Information on doses for drugs licensed for use in animals should be obtained from the IVS or the manufacturer’s data sheet. The information here is intended as a guide only. Remember, unlicensed drugs are given on your responsibility. Remember withholding times in food animals too. The list of drugs available in NZ (and their price!) is subject to change. glibenclamide

sulphonylurea hypoglycaemic

indications

non insulin dependent diabetes

dose

dog 200µg/kg daily po

glipizide

sulphonylurea hypoglycaemic

indications

non insulin dependent diabetes

dose

dog 250 - 500µg/kg, cat 500µg/kg po twice daily with food - try it for 4-6 weeks: if the animal doesn't respond after 6 weeks it is unlikely ever to, so start insulin as the animal is probably a Type I diabetic.

glycerol

osmotic diuretic

indications

glaucoma

pharmacokinetics

Has some effect in 10 mins lasts 5 hours

side effects

vomiting

care

diabetes

dose

dog 0.5ml/kg po every 8 hours

glyceryl trinitrate

nitrate vasodilator

indications

to reduce arterial blood pressure intraoperatively, congestive heart failure

pharmacokinetics

iv infusion - onset 3 minutes, duration 11 mins. metabolised in blood and tissues to cyanide which is converted in the liver to thiocyanate and eliminated in the urine, faeces and exhaled. Solutions are unstable and must be protected from light (wrap drip bag & giving set in aluminium foil)

side effects

hypotension in overdose.(give dobutamine infusion), (cyanide toxicity at very high dose rates)

dose

dog 0.5 - 10 µg/kg/min iv infusion to achieve the desired ABP. Maximum dose 0.75 mg/kg/hour (cyanide toxicity)

note

Do not use iv unless ABP is being monitored continuously

glycopyrrolate

antimuscarinic

indications

treatment of bradycardia

side effects

tachycardia

dose

dog & cat 2 - 8µg/kg iv

horse 1 - 3µg/kg iv

griseofulvin

antifungal

indications

ringworm

contra-indications

liver disease, pregnancy

side effects

liver toxicity in cats

dose

dog and cat 15 - 20mg/kg daily po

horse 10mg/kg daily po

cattle 7.5mg/kg daily po

treatment is usually required for 3 - 4 but possibly up to 12 weeks.

note

women should handle with care - teratogenic

guaiphenesin

(guaifenesin USAN, INN; glyceryl guaiacolate; guaiacol glycerol ether; GG)

muscle realaxant and expectorant

indications

muscle relaxation during anaesthesia

care

perivascular injection is very irritant; concentrations greater than 5% will cause haemolysis, particularly in dogs and cattle

dose

horses (premedicated): 50 - 100mg/kg iv with thiopentone or ketamine

other spp.: 30 - 100mg/kg iv to effect followed by induction agent
