Information on doses for drugs licensed for use in animals should be obtained from the IVS or the manufacturer’s data sheet. The information here is intended as a guide only. Remember, unlicensed drugs are given on your responsibility. Remember withholding times in food animals too. The list of drugs available in NZ (and their price!) is subject to change. ranitidine

histamine H2 antagonist

indications

gastric ulcers, gastritis, oesophagitis, prokinetic

side effects

very rare

precautions

increases gastric pH so may alter absorption of some drugs

dose

dogs and cats 0.5 - 2mg/kg 2 - 3 times daily po, sc, im or iv

horses - 6.6mg/kg po three times daily; 1mg/kg iv three times daily

rifampicin

rifamycin type antibiotic

note

Reserved for treatment of TB in people - do not use in animals.

rocuronium

competitive muscle relaxant

indications

muscle relaxation during anaesthesia

contraindications

liver or kidney disease, no facilities for IPPV

dose

dogs & cats - 500µg/kg iv then 200µg/kg increments

romifidine

α2 agonist

excipients

methyl & propyl paraben, saline

indications

sedation & analgesia, premed

contraindications

pregnancy, diabetes, cardiovascular disease

side effects

bradycardia, hyper/hypotension, arrhythmias, hyperglycaemia, reduced temperature, respiratory depression, vomiting.

pharmacokinetics

bioavailability, dog - 86/92% - im/sc; cat - 95% im

Vd dog - 3l/kg; cat 6l/kg

half life dog - 2 hr, cat - 6 hr

elimination 80% in urine

dose

dog - 40 - 120µg/kg iv, im, sc

cat - 200 - 400µg/kg iv, im

horse - 40 - 120µg/kg iv, may be followed 5 mins later by butorphanol 20µg/kg

interactions

potentiated sulphonamides - arrhythmias?

other sedatives, anticonvulsants & opioids: increased sedation
