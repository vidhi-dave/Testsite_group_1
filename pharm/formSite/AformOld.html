Information on doses for drugs licensed for use in animals should be obtained from the IVS or the manufacturer’s data sheet. The information here is intended as a guide only. Remember, unlicensed drugs are given on your responsibility. Remember withholding times in food animals too. The list of drugs available in NZ (and their price!) is subject to change. acetazolamide

carbonic anhydrase inhibitor

indications

glaucoma, (epilepsy)

pharmacokinetics

Absorption - onset of action 30 minutes, peak at 2-4 hours, duration of action 4-8 hours

Metabolism and Elimination - within 24 hours of administration, about 90% of the drug is excreted unchanged by tubular secretion and passive resorption processses

side effects and toxicity

gut disturbances, sedation, depression, excitement, bone marrow depression, crystalluria, dysuria, polyuria, hypokalaemia, hyponatraemia, hyperglycaemia

contraindications

significant hepatic disease, renal or adrenocortical insufficiency, electrolyte imbalance, severe pulmonary obstruction, long term use in chronic non-congestive, angle-closure glaucoma

dose

For adjunctive therapy of glaucoma

Dog 10 - 25 mg/kg po divided 2 - 4 times daily

Cat 50 mg/kg once iv, 7 mg/kg po divided 2 - 3 times daily

atropine

antimuscarinic

indications

anaesthetic premedication in cats (and pigs?)and when irritant anaesthetics like ether are being used, treating gut spasm, treating bradycardia, organophosphate poisoning

contra-indications

glaucoma, tachycardia

precautions

care in cardiac disease, horses - cycloplegia often causes panic, ruminants - blocks parotid secretions but not submandibular - very sticky saliva

Rabbits possess an enzyme which breaks atropine down rapidly - it is too short acting to be of much use in this species.

side effects

dries secretions, reduces salivation, slows gut, tachycardia, dilates pupil, blurred vision, difficulty with urination

dose

anaesthetic premed

all species 20 - 50Âµg/kg sc / im

bradycardia

5 - 20Âµg/kg iv - effects last about 15 mins (glycopyrrolate better if available)

organophosphate poisoning

25 - 200Âµg/kg sc every 3 - 4 hours

azathioprine

(Imuran) immunosuppressant, anticancer drug

indications

long term, moderately potent immunosuppression, to allow reduction in steroid dose

side effects

bone marrow suppression

precautions

A complete blood count should be performed every 10- 14 days for the first 2-3 months of therapy and should be repeated at monthly to bimonthly intervals thereafter. stop drug if marked neutropaenia or thrombocytopaenia develop.

dose

dogs 1-1.5 mg/kg once daily for 2 weeks then every other day as needed

cats 0.3-0.5 mg/kg every other day

takes several weeks to work
