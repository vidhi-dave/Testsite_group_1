Information on doses for drugs licensed for use in animals should be obtained from the IVS or the manufacturer’s data sheet. The information here is intended as a guide only. Remember, unlicensed drugs are given on your responsibility. Remember withholding times in food animals too. The list of drugs available in NZ (and their price!) is subject to change. famotidine

H2 antagonist

indications

gastric ulcers

dose

dog 0.5 - 1mg/kg once or twice daily po or iv

felbamate

anticonvulsant

pharmacokinetics

dog - half life 5 - 6 h

side effects

tremor (very rare)

dose

dog - 15 - 65 mg/kg

therapeutic plasma levels

15 - 100 µg/ml

fentanyl

µ opioid agonist

indications

severe pain, usually intra-op

side effects

respiratory depression, muscle rigidity, bradycardia

dose

1 - 5µg/kg every 20 mins iv; or, iv infusion of 3 - 10µg/kg/hr; or, transdermal patch as necessary

fludrocortisone

mineralocorticoid

indications

mineralocorticoid replacement in adrenocortical insufficiency

side effects

sodium and potassium imbalances

dose

dogs & cats 15 -20µg/kg po daily

note

animals may also need glucocorticoid replacement

flumazenil

benzodiazepine antagonist

indications

reversing benzodiazepine overdose

contra-indications

epilepsy

side effects

short acting - danger of relapse

dose

4 - 8µg/kg slow iv to effect, max dose 15µg/kg

note

too expensive to use

flushing solutions

see Tris - EDTA flushing solutions

fluoroscein

fluorescent dye

indications

diagnosis of corneal damage, latency of lacrymal ducts

side effects

bacterial contamination unless single use ampoules are used

dose

one or two drops onto the cornea

note

potent dye - keep off clothes!

fluoxetine

(Prozac)

selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor antidepressant

indications

obsessive compulsive behaviour, dominance aggression, urine spraying

dose

dogs: 0.5 - 1mg/kg daily

cats: 0.5 - 4mg/cat

frusemide

(furosemide USAN, INN)

loop diuretic

indications

general diuresis: congestive cardiomyopathy, pulmonary oedema, hypercaluric nephropathy, uraemia, hyperkalaemia, occasionally for hypertension. cattle- post-parturient udder oedema, (horses - to help reduce epistaxis by depleting circulating blood volume further)(one of the main drugs used illegally to slow racehorses)

pharmacokinetics

Absorption iv onset in 5 minutes, peak effect in 30 minutes, duration 2 hours

oral onset in 1 hour, peaks at 1-2 hours, duration 6 hours

Distribution highly protein bound - 95 %

Metabolism half life 15 min - 2 hours, may be increased in renal failure, uraemia, congestive heart failure and neonates

Elimination small fraction filtered by the glomerulus, rest secreted into the proximal renal tubules by an organic anion pump which is inhibited by probenecid

side effects

overdosage leading to dehydration (decreases clearance of concurrent administered drugs), hypokalaemia which may be predisposed to by anorexia, hyponatraemia , tolerance develops

care

pre-existing electrolyte imbalances or conditions that may lead to these eg. vomiting, diarrhoea, coincidental therapy with aminoglycosides, tetracyclines, cephaloridine can increase proximal convoluted tubule nephrotoxicity

dose

dog & cat general diuretic and cardiogenic or pulmonary oedema 2-4 mg/kg po 3 times daily - every 2-3 days, 2-4 mg/kg iv, can repeat every 4 hours

hypercalcaemia / hypercaluric nephropathy - make sure patient is well hydrated, 5 mg/kg iv bolus, then infusion of 5 mg/kg/hour

acute renal failure/uraemia-replace fluid deficit, 2 mg/kg iv -if no diuresis in 1 hour give 4 mg/kg, if no response increase to 6 mg/kg

hyperkalaemia - 2 mg/kg iv if mannitol has not worked in 1 hour

ascites - 1 - 2 mg/kg po or sc once or twice daily

hypertension - 1 - 2 mg/kg po twice daily

acute heart failure with respiratory oedema - 4 - 7 mg/kg iv every 1 - 2 hours
