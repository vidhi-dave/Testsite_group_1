Remember, unlicensed drugs are given on your responsibility. Remember withholding times in food animals too. The list of drugs available in NZ (and their price!) is subject to change. digoxin

cardiac glycoside

indications

congestive heart failure, atrial fibrillation

side effects

anorexia, vomiting, arrhythmias

pharmacokinetics

Absorption may be decreased by food. Time to peak plasma concentrations vary depending on the formulation and dose schedule - if initial dose may take 6-8 hours to peak - elixir - 45 - 60 minutes, - tablet - 90 minutes

Distribution approximately 20% bound to serum proteins (species dependent) and the remainder is free in the serum. Strongly bound to skeletal muscle but is also distributed widely, with the highest concentration in kidney, heart, intestine, liver and skeletal muscle. Lowest concentrations are found in the plasma and brain.

Metabolism Approximately 15% is metabolised by the liver. In some people, metabolism by gut bacteria is important, and antibiotic induced changes in gut bacteria may lead to digoxin toxicity. Dog half life varies between 14 - 56 hours, cat is 33 - 58 hours.

Elimination Remaining 85% is excreted renally by glomerular filtration and tubular secretion

contraindications

digitalis intoxication, ventricular fibrillation, pericardial disease, hypertrophic cardiomyopathy

care

renal failure, lung disease

dose

dogs 220µg/m2 or 5 - 10µg/kg twice daily

rapid digitalisation (not recommended) total dose equals 0.01 - 0.02 mg/kg, give 1/2 the dose slowly iv, wait 30 - 60 minutes and give 1/4 doseslowly IV, wait a further 30 -60 minutes and give the rest if necessary

cats 7 - 15µg/kg every other day or 4µg/kg once daily

horses 10µg/kg twice daily

therapeutic plasma levels

Dog 1.0 - 2.5 ng/ml

Cat - 0.9 - 2.0 ng/ml

Mild toxicity 2.5 - 6 ng/ml. Severe toxicity > 6 ng/ml.

interactions

do not give with quinidine

doxorubicin

anticancer antibiotic

indications

lymphoma, sarcomas, carcinomas and leukaemia; usually in combinations

pharmacokinetics

must be given iv. Metabolised in liver to active and inactive metabolites. Elimination half life in dogs 17h, 32 h for metabolites, excretion mainly in the faeces.

side effects

vomiting, diarrhoea, leukopaenia, thrombocytopaenia, cardiotoxicity, allergic reactions and, in cats, nephrotoxicity. Very irritant if injected perivascularly

contra-indications

liver disease

dose

dogs 30 mg/m2 iv over 10min into fast flow of saline

cats 20 mg/m2 iv

Usually given every 3 weeks. Cumulative dose should be less than 150 mg/m2 (cats) and 200 mg/m2 (dogs)

note

A nasty drug - handle with care. Care with faeces and urine of treated animals.
