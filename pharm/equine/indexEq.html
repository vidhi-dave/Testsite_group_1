Equine Clinical Pharmacology

Introduction

Equine clinical pharmacology is the study of drugs in equine clinical cases. When armed with a source of current information about the rational use of drugs, including best practice principles, veterinarians should be able to adopt a rational approach to drug therapy in clinical cases. Vital information needed when devising a pharmacological treatment protocol includes: body system(s) affected, pathophysiology of the disease, best practice guidelines, dosage of drugs, and precautions and potential adverse effects of drugs.

Learning Objectives

At the end of these sessions, you should be able to decide on a rational course of treatment for common conditions in the horse

Organisation

All students are expected to work through all scenarios outlined below. You are expected to analyse the animal's problems, decide what the priorities are and justify your choice of treatment. This is more a matter of logical thought than memorising pharmacology. Do not worry about giving a polished presentation - this is not expected of you.

At the start of the session, you will be asked to work in groups of four and given 15 minutes to decide on how you will present each case. Groups will then be selected randomly to outline their approach to the cases presented in each scenario. The audience is expected to ask intelligent questions (!) and provide suggestions where appropriate. Remember, when treating animals, there is not usually a single right answer, just a series of options, some of which are better than others!
