Antibiotics

The wound is likely to be contaminated with a variety of bacteria so a broad spectrum drug is needed.

  • penicillin sc is narrow spectrum and unsuitable.
  • amoxycillin sc is likely to kill a wide range of bacteria except beta lactamase producers.
  • co-amoxyclav sc kills most bacteria and is the drug of choice.
  • enrofloxacin sc does not kill Streps, and Staphs quickly develop resistance. Significant resistance may develop - do not use.
  • gentamicin sc will only kill G- and some Staphs. It does not penetrate tissues well.
  • co-trimoxazole sc is broad spectrum but is inactivated in pus.
  • cephazolin im kills a wide range of bacteria but significant resistance could occur - do not use.
  • clindamycin po is narrow spectrum and is usually reserved for anaerobic or rickettsial infections - do not use.
  • metronidazole sc only kills anaerobes and is unsuitable.
