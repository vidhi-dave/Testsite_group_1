Morphine, medetomidine and maropitant combinations for analgesia in dogs.

Summary

Morphine, medetomidine and maropitant are potentially synergistic as analgesics. This study will compare Glasgow pain scores postoperatively in normal bitch spay cases after combinations given just before surgery. Blood samples will be taken at intervals to measure plasma concentrations of the three drugs to attempt PKPD modelling. Mechanical pain thresholds will be measured post op to complement pain scoring.

Since morphine and morphine/medetomidine combinations are already used routinely in anaesthesia, we consider this trial low risk.

Potential problems

Morphine and medetomidine have a well established risk profile (respiratory depression and bradycardia / hypotension) which are routinely treated during anaesthesia.

Maropitant can cause pain on sc injection and increase liver enzymes after several days of high doses orally. Similar drugs can cause a variety of side effects in people, mostly minor. These have not been reported in dogs. Maropitant is licensed in the EU for treating post op vomiting caused by morphine: no adverse effects have been reported.

The most likely adverse effect is lack of efficacy. In this case the dogs will be given rescue analgesia.
