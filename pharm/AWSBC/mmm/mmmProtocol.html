Protocol

Inclusion criteria

Healthy female dogs more than 10 Kg arriving at MUVTH for routine ovariohysterectomy.

Premedication - acepromazine (0.02mg/kg IM) about 40 minutes prior to anaesthetic induction.

Exclusion criteria

Aggressive dogs unlikely to sedate on acepromazine alone.

Weight under 10kg.

Dogs on other medication.

Higher than usual anaesthetic risk, eg, brachycephalics.

Treatments groups and dose rates

Group 1: Morphine 0.6mg/kg IM (n= 10)

Group 2: Morphine 0.3 mg/kg IM + Maropitant 1 mg/kg IM (n=10)

Group 3: Morphine 0.3 mg/kg IM + Dexmedetomidine 10 g/kg IM (n=10)

Group 4: Morphine 0.2 mg/kg IM + Dexmedetomidine 7 g /kg IM+ Maropitant 0.7 mg/kg IM (n=10)

Time of administration of treatment drugs

About 30 min before expected time of start of onset of surgery. This may be around anaesthesia induction time.

Post-operative pain assessment

1) Short form of the Glasgow composite pain scale (CMPS),

2) a mechanical nociceptive threshold testing device, and

3) a visual analogue scale (VAS)

To be carried out by Sandeep

Frequency

At extubation, 15, 30, 60, 120, 180, 360 and 540 minutes after extubation.

Blood sampling

2 mL blood will be collected at 15 min, 30 min, 60 min, 120 min after test drug administration (total 8 mL).

Intraoperative monitoring data

Routine anaesthesia monitoring: heart rate, respiration rate, oxygen saturation (SpO2) and blood pressure (every five minutes, plus end-tidal concentration of isoflurane if possible) will be taken from the standard anaesthesia record.

Rescue analgesia

If any dog has a CMPS score of > 6 rescue analgesia with morphine 0.3mg/kg IM ± fentanyl 2µg/kg IV will be given.

Adverse effects

Any potential adverse effects will be noted on the anaesthesia or recovery form.

Other

Any other treatment the anaesthetist considers clinically necessary should be given and recorded.
