Opioids pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Usually given im or iv. Oral absorption is variable - morphine is about 20% bioavailable. Since the major action is in the spinal cord, opioids are sometimes given intrathecally or epidurally to get direct to the site of action (and stay there). Morphine is best by these routes, but transfer to the brain in the CSF can lead to prolonged respiratory depression. Fentanyl will cross intact skin and is sometimes used as transdermal patches. Methadone is very poorly absorbed after oral administration in dogs.

Distribution

There are large differences in fat solubility; morphine is relatively hydrophilic, fentanyl derivatives are relatively lipophilic. Drugs which distribute more rapidly to the brain have a higher abuse potential, eg, heroin.

Metabolism

Morphine is conjugated with glucuronide to produce morphine 3 glucuronide (M3G) and M6G (potent analgesic) Remember that cats do not possess glucuronyl transferase! One dose is enough in this species. Methadone is metabolised slowly in people, but seems similar to morphine in most domestic animals.

Elimination

Large differences between drugs and species. Remember cats! Many opioids are excreted in the bile and undergo enterohepatic recirculation. Beware when darting wild animals and returning to the wild.
