Co-transmitters

Adenosine triphosphate (ATP) is a major component of the vesicles containing noradrenaline which are released on sympathetic activiation. It is thought to be responsible for the fast component of sympathetic responses, with noradrenaline having similar but slower effects.

Neuropeptide Y, acting at NPY receptors, is probably usually released with noradrenaline too.

Other co-transmitters include: vasoactive intestinal peptide, gonadotrophin releasing hormone, 5 HT, GABA, and dopamine. Many more substances are also implicated.
