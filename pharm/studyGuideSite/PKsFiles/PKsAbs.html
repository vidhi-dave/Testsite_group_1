Absorption
• pharmacokinetics consists of four processes: absorption, distribution, metabolism and elimination.
• most drugs must be absorbed to act.
• iv administration bypasses drug absorption
• absorption depends on lipid solubility and ionisation
• drugs are often formulated to provide delayed absorption
• bioavailability gives an indication of the extent of absorption

Absorption

This is the process that involves the drug moving from its site of administration into the blood. A major factor here is the route of administration. Once the drug is administered, it must dissolve in the body fluids to be absorbed. This step is manipulated by altering the formulation of the drug. After dissolution, the drug must move across cell membranes into the circulation in most cases. Intravenous administration bypasses the absorption phase.
