Basic toxicology
  • Stabilise the animal – Provide symptomatic care to ensure that the animal’s functions are maintained
  • Limit Exposure – remove animal from poisoned environment
  • Limit absorption – apply decontamination principles to remove poison from site of exposure
  • Promote elimination-fluid diuresis may remove some poisons via the urine.
  • Identify the poison-whenever possible try to identify the poison, have owner bring in container or sample of poison but caution owner to use gloves, sealed containers or any method of transport to prevent poisoning to themselves.

    • Basic toxicology

    Toxicology is the study of poisons. Almost anything can be toxic if given in sufficient quantities which is the reason for the saying: “THE DOSE MAKES THE POISON”. Toxicology and pharmacology are disciplines that are opposite ends of a spectrum. Pharmacology attempts to use compounds for a therapeutic effect while toxicology studies compounds that have a harmful effect. In iatrogenic toxicity, the therapeutic effect of a medicine, possible due to a miscalculation of the dose, becomes an adverse or toxic effect.
