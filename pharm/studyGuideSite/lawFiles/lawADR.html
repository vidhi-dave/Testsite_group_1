Adverse drug reactions

An adverse drug reaction is an undesirable, unintended or unexpected response to the clinical use of a drug, including injury, toxicity, sensitivity reaction or lack of efficacy.

possible causes of adverse reactions:

  • anaphylactic / anaphylactoid reactions
  • misreading instructions - double dosing, incorrect dilution, miscalculation
  • use of an unlabelled product
  • known side effects of a drug
  • use of expired product
  • interactions of one or more animal remedies
  • idiosyncratic reactions

Adverse drug reactions should be reported to the ACVM Group on forms provided by them. If you do not have a form handy, collect as much information as possible at the time and organise the form later. Or contact NZFSA ACVM Group, PO Box 2526, Wellington. Fax: (04) 460 8771 phone (04) 460 8750

The info you will need is likely to include at least:

  • Your name and contact details
  • The name, batch no., registration no. and expiry date of the product
  • The animal’s owner’s name and contact details
  • The actual dose given and the route of administration
  • The number of animals treated and the number reacting
  • The date and nature of the reaction
  • Animal details: species / breed, age, sex, weight
  • Other products given
  • Immediate treatment
  • Samples taken for analysis

Keep any remaining drug for analysis!

Reporting of adverse reactions is likely to become a condition of registration of all veterinary medicines, ie, compulsory. Reactions in people handling the drugs should also be reported to the ACVM Group.

As a matter of professional courtesy, the drug company should also be informed. Adverse reactions to off label use of drugs should also be reported.
