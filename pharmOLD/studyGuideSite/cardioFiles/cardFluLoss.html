Fluid loss

Fluids can be lost directly by a variety of routes, or there can be a relative loss through vasodilatation (see shock). Common routes of fluid loss:

  • haemorrhage (not necessarily external)
  • vomiting
  • diarrhoea
  • not drinking
  • anaesthesia (breathing dry gas)
  • laparotomy / thoracotomy (evaporation)
  • burns

The differents routes of loss will result in other things apart from water being lost, for instance, excessive bleeding will cause loss of RBCs and clotting factors which will have to be replaced.

The body will adjust the fluid compartments (slowly) to try to maintain blood volume to preserve blood flow to the vital tissues (brain, heart and kidneys). Eventually homoeostasis breaks down and shock results.
