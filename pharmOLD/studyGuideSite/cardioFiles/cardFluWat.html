Water in the body

The distribution of body water (very approximate figures):

  • total body water 65% body weight (adults), 80% body weight (neonates)
  • intracellular fluid 45% bw
  • extracellular fluid 20% bw
  • blood volume 9% bw
  • plasma volume 5% bw

In very fat animals these figures will be lower.

The blood is the only fluid which can be resuscitated directly. Composition of blood:

plasma
water
proteins
albumin
globulin
clotting factors
electrolytes
glucose
waste products
cells
red blood cells
white blood cells
platelets

Problems can arise from deficiences or (rarely) excesses of any of these components.
