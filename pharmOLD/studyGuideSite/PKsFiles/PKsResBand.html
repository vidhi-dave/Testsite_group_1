Residues - banned drugs

Some drugs have been banned in food animals because of the risk of residues.
The method of “banning” varies from place to place: in NZ it is not illegal to give the drugs in table (a) but it is illegal for the farmer to move the animals or present them for slaughter when they contain the drugs. This gives rise to anomalies: clenbuterol is licensed for delaying parturition in cows, but as a b agonist is banned in food animals.
The drugs in table (b) are likely to be banned soon unless tracking and tagging programmes are instituted for them.
