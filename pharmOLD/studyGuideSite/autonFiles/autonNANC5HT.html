5 hydroxytryptamine

5HT (serotonin) is used to regulate smooth muscle, particularly in the gut and cardiovascular system, and as a neurotransmitter in the CNS. It also regulates platelet aggregation.
Its pharmacology is complicated by the fact that there are at least 14 different 5HT receptor subtypes, and many drugs which act at some of these but are not specific. There also seem to be species differences. Synthesis, storage, release and uptake are similar to noradrenaline (at least in the CNS); somatostatin, substance P and vasoactive intestinal peptide are probably co-transmitters.

Drugs used clinically for their effect on 5HT receptors include ondansetron, a 5HT3 antagonist used as an antiemetic; metaclopramide, a 5HT4 antagonist used as a gut prokinetic (see gut notes) and many antidepressants, such as fluoxetine (Prozac) used to modify behaviour (see CNS notes). Very many other drugs affect 5HT receptors as a side effect.
