The eye

Most drugs given for ocular problems are instilled onto the cornea as drops or ointment (although if high doses are given they can be absorbed and produce systemic effects). Occasionally subconjunctival injections are made - usually to provide a depot of drug, particularly in large animals. Systemic administration of drugs is only used in serious cases. Smart delivery systems such as drug impreganated contact lenses (in people) and various plastic implants (in animals) are starting to be used.

Definitions

mydriasis - dilatation of the pupil
