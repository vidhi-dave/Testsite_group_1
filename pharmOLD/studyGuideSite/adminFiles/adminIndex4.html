195.409 Aim

To provide a scientific basis for the rational use of drugs in animals.

Course Objectives

By the end of this course you should:

  • have a sound understanding of the effects, mechanism of action and uses of the major groups of drugs used to treat animals which will allow you to use these drugs safely and effectively
  • be aware of drugs which are likely to be used in the near future
  • know how and where to obtain further information on drugs
  • have a sound understanding of the principles of pharmacokinetics and how these are used to ensure that an animal receives the correct dose of drug
  • be able to evaluate scientific and clinical reports of drug trials and apply this knowledge to veterinary practice
  • know the law relating to the veterinary use of drugs in New Zealand and the responsibilities of the veterinary surgeon.
