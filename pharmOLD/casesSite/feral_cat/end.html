Treatment of choice
In this case, it is probably best to heavily sedate the cat with ketamine (± diazepam) to examine the wound. The wound should be cleaned and stitched if necessary - remember that getting the stitches out again may be tricky. Neutering should also be considered at this stage. It is probably best to give a depot injection of long acting antibiotic as well. The carers should be told to keep the cat in a cage and check it without handling for 3 - 4 days at least.
