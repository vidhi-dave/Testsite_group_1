Information on doses for drugs licensed for use in animals should be obtained from the IVS or the manufacturer’s data sheet. The information here is intended as a guide only. Remember, unlicensed drugs are given on your responsibility. Remember withholding times in food animals too. The list of drugs available in NZ (and their price!) is subject to change. nalbuphine

opioid partial agonist

indications

moderate pain, anaesthetic premed

dose

dogs 0.15 - 0.3mg/kg sc / im / iv

naloxone

opioid antagonist

indications

opioid overdose

side effects

short acting, possibility of relapse, tachycardia in man

dose

dogs 0.04 - 1mg/kg sc, im, iv

people in emergencies 100 - 200µg iv, if reponse inadequate, increments of 100µg every two minutes, further doses by im injection after 1 - 2 hours if necessary

note

expensive

nandrolone

anabolic steroid

indications

stimulation of erythropoiesis, promotes body tissue building and reverses catabolism, adjunct to treatment of renal failure

dose

All species the general dose is 1mg/kg im up to:

Horse up to 200 mg total dose at one time

Dog up to 40 mg at one time

Cat up to 20 mg

given every 1 - 3 weeks

notes

Do not give to competition animals or food animals.

The most widely abused anabolic steroid in human athletes.
