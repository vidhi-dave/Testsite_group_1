Information on doses for drugs licensed for use in animals should be obtained from the IVS or the manufacturer’s data sheet. The information here is intended as a guide only. Remember, unlicensed drugs are given on your responsibility. Remember withholding times in food animals too. The list of drugs available in NZ (and their price!) is subject to change. Errors and omissions, email me.


enalapril

angiotensin converting enzyme inhibitor

indications

congestive heart failure

side effects

hypotension, azotaemia

precautions

monitor renal function

dose

dogs 500µg/kg po 1 (- 2) times daily

cats 250 - 500µg/kg 1 (- 2) times daily

ephedrine

adrenergic α1 agonist

indications

urinary incontinence

side effects

Crosses blood brain barrier to cause stimulation, vasoconstriction

care

heart disease

dose

dog 12.5 - 50mg po 2 - 3 times daily

note

use pseudoephedrine instead - fewer central effects

Both ephedrine and pseudoephedrine can be precursors for methamphetamine and are likely to be controlled drugs soon.

Epsom salts

magnesium sulphate

osmotic laxative

indications

constipation

dose

dogs 5 - 25g po

cats 2 - 5g po

horses 1g/kg in 4L water po

cattle 250 - 500g po

pigs 25 - 125g po

erythromycin

gut prokinetic (and macrolide antibiotic)

indications

increase gut motility, infections

spectrum

Gram +

break point

0.25µg/mL for Streps, 0.5µg/mL for others

side effects

vomiting & diarrhoea (nausea / anorexia)

foals hyperthermia, respiratory distress

dose

prokinetic

dog and cat 0.5 - 1 mg/kg po three times daily after food

antibiotic

10 - 20mg/kg po 3 times daily

foals R. equi 25 mg/kg po 3 - 4 times daily

erythropoietin

(EPO)

stimulates production of red blood cells

The commercial preparation is recombinant human EPO.

indications

PCV < 20%

contra-indications

hypertension

side effects

hypersensitive allergic reactions can occur, especially in horses

dose

dog & cat 100 IU/kg given iv or sc every 2-3 days initially for several weeks

note

Animals should respond in 2-6 weeks. Prohibitively expensive at the moment. The use of anabolic steroids may enhance the effect on haematopoiesis. Abused by human athletes. Horses produce antibodies to it - counterproductive.

ethinyloestradiol

synthetic orally active oestrogen

indications

urinary incontinence in bitches, prostatic hyperplasia and anal adenomata in male dogs

side effects

bone marrow suppression, oestrus, feminisation, pyometra in entire bitches

pharmacokinetics

ethinyl substitution prevents first pass metabolism so effects similar to injected oestradiol

dose

as low as possible

male dogs - 50 - 100µg daily

bitches - up to 1mg daily for 3 days then up to 1mg weekly

ethylestrenol

orally active anabolic steroid with high anabolic: androgenic ratio (19:1).

pharmacokinetics

absorbed 1-3 hours; negligible hepatic metabolism (no glucuronide conjugation); renal excretion is rapid and predictable

dose

horse - haemopoiesis and appetite stimulation - 33µg/kg po daily, convalescence and repair - 66µg/kg po daily

note

Do not use in racehorses.

Effects will take at least a week to appear

etomidate

iv anaesthetic

indications

induction of anaesthesia

care

can cause excitement on induction and recovery in unpremedicated animals, no analgesia, depresses adrenals

dose

dogs & cats (premedicated): 0.5 - 1mg/kg iv
