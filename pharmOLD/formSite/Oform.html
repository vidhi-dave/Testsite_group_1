Information on doses for drugs licensed for use in animals should be obtained from the IVS or the manufacturer’s data sheet. The information here is intended as a guide only. Remember, unlicensed drugs are given on your responsibility. Remember withholding times in food animals too. The list of drugs available in NZ (and their price!) is subject to change. oestrogen tablets

indications

bitches - bladder sphincter incompetance, misalliance; male dogs - prostatic hyperplasia, anal ademomata

side effects

bone marrow depression, pyometra

dose

bitch 0.1 - 1mg/kg every day for 3 - 5 days then up to 1mg/week

male dog 50 - 100µg daily

Use lowest dose that works.

availability

many human preparations but “Premarin” is probably most suitable as it is available in low dose tablets (0.3mg, 0.625mg, 1.25mg)

note

see also ethinyloestradiol

omeprazole

proton pump inhibitor

indications

gastric ulceration, oesophagitis, gastrinoma

dose

dogs 0.5 - 1mg/kg once daily po

cats 0.7mg/kg once daily po

horses 4mg/kg once daily po for treatment, 2mg/kg once daily po for prevention

ondansetron

5HT3 antagonist antiemetic

indications

vomiting

dose

dog and cat 0.1 - 0.2mg/kg sc three times daily po or 0.5mg/kg/h by iv infusion

oxazepam

(Serepax) benzodiazepine sedative - controlled drug C5

indications

appetite stimulant

side effects

sedation

dose

cats 2.5 mg/cat po bid

oxytocin

increases tone in uterus

indications

to promote uterine involution and reduce bleeding from the uterine lining,to induce parturition in sows with uterine atony

dose

cow 10 - 40 iu im

sow 2 - 10 iu im

mare 10 - 40 iu im

ewe 2 - 10 iu im
