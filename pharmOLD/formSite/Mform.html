Information on doses for drugs licensed for use in animals should be obtained from the IVS or the manufacturer’s data sheet. The information here is intended as a guide only. Remember, unlicensed drugs are given on your responsibility. Remember withholding times in food animals too. The list of drugs available in NZ (and their price!) is subject to change. magnesium sulphate

see Epsom salts

mannitol

osmotic diuretic

indications

cerebral oedema, glaucoma

contraindications

heart disease - acts as a colloid to raise venous pressure

precautions

will cause sloughing if given perivascularly

dose

dog 1g/kg slowly iv and repeat as necessary if diuresis occurs

medroxyprogesterone

progestin

indications

inappropriate marking in cats, male aggression in dogs

side effects

increase in appetite and weight (25%), decrease in appetite and weight (10%), depression, lethargy, cystic endometrial hyperplasia, mammary hypertrophy, diabetogenic stress, increase blood glucose, retards hair growth, hair loss and colour change over injection site, mammary carcinoma (but not usually all at once!)

dose

cats 10 to 20 mg/kg or 50 mg (queens) to 100 mg (males) per cat. May be repeated at 3 months

dogs 2 - 4 mg/kg sc repeat every 6 to 12 months

megestrol

progestin

indications

inappropriate marking in cats

side effects

increase in appetite and weight (25%), decrease in appetite and weight (10%), depression, lethargy, cystic endometrial hyperplasia, mammary hypertrophy, diabetogenic stress, increase blood glucose, retards hair growth, hair loss and colour change over injection site, mammary carcinoma (but not usually all at once!)

dose

2 to 4 mg/kg po on a quickly tapering regime - once daily for 4 days, once every second day for 8 days, twice weekly for 4 weeks and once weekly thereafter. Use for periods greater than two months is not recommended or 5mg/kg/day followed by 2.5mg/kg/day for 2 weeks

melphalan

(Alkeran) anticancer alkylating agent

indication

multiple myeloma

side effects

bone marrow suppression, anorexia, vomiting.

dose

1.5 mg/m2 po daily (dogs and cats)

metoclopramide

antiemetic (D2 antagonist) and gut prokinetic (5HT4 agonist)

indications

vomiting and ileus

dose

antiemetic

dogs and cats 0.5 - 1mg/kg 1 - 2 times daily po, sc, im or iv

prokinetic

dog 0.1 - 1mg/kg three times daily by mouth or 10 - 20µg/kg/hr iv infusion

metformin

biguanide hypoglycaemic

indications

non insulin dependent diabetes

dose

dog 250 - 500mg twice daily po with food

methadone

µ opioid agonist - controlled drug B3

indications

severe pain

dose

dogs - 100 - 500µg/kg sc, im or iv, every 4 - 6hr

horses - 220µg/kg sc or im, 110µg/kg iv

methimazole

not available in NZ - use carbimazole instead.

methotrexate

(Methoblastin) antimetabolite anticancer drug

indications

lymphoma

side effects

Commonly vomiting, nausea and diarrhoeat. Rarely bone marrow suppression

dose

dogs and cats 2.5 mg/m2 po every second day

mexilitine

Class 1b antiarrhythmic

indications

ventricular arrhythmias especially those non-responsive to conventional therapy

arrhythmias induced by digitalis or other drugs

pharmacokinetics

Absorption well absorbed

Metabolism low first pass hepatic transformation

side effects

gut and CNS reactions which are reversible

contraindications

care when there is sinus node dysfunction, conduction defects, bradycardia, hypotension or cardiac, hepatic or renal failure

dose

dog -5 - 8mg/kg 2 - 3 times daily - increase if necessary

notes

Similar to lignocaine but has more side effects (in people). Useful in combination with atenolol in dogs. Rarely used.

midazolam

benzodiazepine sedative - controlled drug C5

indications

sedation in intensive care & ruminants, anaesthetic adjunct

contra-indications

severe liver disease

side effects

excitement in normal dogs, cats & horses

dose

all spp 100 - 200µg/kg iv

milrinone

phosphodiesterase inotrope

indications

congestive heart failure

pharmacokinetics

Absorption dog - oral doses - effects seen within 15 - 30 min which last approximately 4 hours

Metabolism dog - half life 2-3 hrs

Side effects

ventricular arrhthymias in < 5% of dogs with myocardial failure.

dose

dog - initial oral dose of 0.75 mg/kg - may be effective at 12 hour intervals but some dogs do not respond well and may need to be dosed every 6 or 8 hours.

- iv bolus of 30 - 300 µg/kg followed by an infusion of 1 - 10 µg/kg/min

misoprostol

prostaglandin E1 analogue

indications

gastric ulceration, especially that caused by NSAIDs

side effects

diarrhoea, gut pain

contra-indications

pregnancy

dose

dog 2 - 5µg/kg thre times daily po

mitotane

(o,p’ DDD) adrenal cortex cytotoxic

indications

Cushing’s syndrome

pharmacokinetics

Absorption - Poor and variable in dogs. Best when tablets are crushed up in oily food but this exposes the owner to the drug. Very fat soluble - taken up with fat.

Distribution - To fat for storage - leaches out slowly.

Metabolism - Dogs are thought to handle it in a similar way to people - oxidised by P450 providing opportunities for other drugs to interfere.

Elimination - Metabolised drug excreted in the urine, the rest passes through the gut unchanged. Elimination is prolonged in chronic administration.

side effects

overdose will cause Addison’s disease (low corticosteroids). Some dogs will develop Addison’s even at low doses. This often kills dogs. Steroid supplementation may be necessary.

anorexia and vomiting, lethargy and ataxia

dose

dog 50mg/kg for a week then 50mg/kg every 1 - 2 weeks

cat - 25 - 37.5mg/kg/day for 2 months then 12.5 - 50mg/kg/week Give with food

references

see Kintzer, P.P. & Peterson, M.E. (1995) Mitotane (o,p’ DDD) treatment of hyperadrenocorticism in dogs. in Kirk’s Current Veterinary Therapy XII, p416

morphine

opioid analgesic - controlled drug B1

indications

severe pain, anaesthetic premedication

side effects

excitment at high doses in cats and horses

respiratory depression in combination with anaesthetics

precautions

raises intracranial pressure

may not work well in ruminants

avoid iv in dogs (histamine relase) (very slow infusion acceptable)

dose

all spp - 0.1 - 1mg/kg sc / im

note

Effects of overdose in cats and horses can look like pain.
