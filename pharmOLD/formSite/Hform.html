Information on doses for drugs licensed for use in animals should be obtained from the IVS or the manufacturer’s data sheet. The information here is intended as a guide only. Remember, unlicensed drugs are given on your responsibility. Remember withholding times in food animals too. The list of drugs available in NZ (and their price!) is subject to change. heparin

parenteral anticoagulant

indications

maintaining iv catheter patency, prevention of clotting in response to other intravascular hardware, venous thrombosis, disseminated intravascular coagulation

side effects

bleeding, hypersensitivity reactions

contraindications

liver disease, haemorrhage (except DIC)

caution

do not give im - haematoma formation. iv is best.

dose

catheters - flush with 5units / ml crystalloid solution

dog - 100 - 200 units /kg slowly iv then 50 units / kg every 3 hours or as necessary or 40 - 80 units /kg sc 3 times daily

cat - 200 units /kg sc 3 times daily

treatment of overdose

protamine sulphate 1mg/100units heparin given by slow iv injection

hyaluronic acid

anti-arthritic

indications

mild - moderate joint disease

side effects

Local reaction post-injection. Should resolve in 48 - 96 hours

precautions

aseptic technique required

dose

horse 2 ml/joint intra-articularly once a week, can be given iv at 4 ml/horse. withdraw at least 48 hours before racing

dog 3-5 mg/joint intra-articularly once a week

hydralazine

arterial vasodilator

indications

mitral regurgitation and left sided heart failure

side effects

reflex tachycardia, hypotension, gut disturbances, anorexia

pharmacokinetics

Absorption rapid after oral administration; onset of action within I hour, peaks at 3 - 5 hours, duration of action 11 - 13 hours

Distribution wide distribution; humans is 85% plasma protein bound

Metabolism extensively metabolised in the liver; uraemia affects biotransformation and may cause serum concentrations to rise in renal failure

Elimination renal with about 15% unchanged

side-effects

severe hypotension - introduce the drug slowly

sodium and water retention - combine with a loop diuretic

reflex tachycardia and increase in automaticity

anorexia and vomiting - infrequent in dogs, more frequent in cats

contraindications

hypovolaemia

care

renal disease, autoimmune disease

dose

dog - titrate to effect. Start at 0.5 mg/kg, monitor blood pressure and increase as needed up to 2mg/kg twice daily

cat - initially 2.5 mg (total dose) and can titrate up to 5mg twice daily

hydrochlorthiazide

thiazide diuretic

indications

general diuresis, diabetes insipidus

side effects

hypokalaemia

dose

dog and cat 2 - 4 mg/kg po twice daily

hydrocortisone

(cortisol)

glucocorticoid

indications

rapid anti-inflammatory effect, glucocorticoid replacement, shock?

side effects

abortion (all the corticosteroid effects if used long term)

dose

dogs 1 - 10mg/kg iv, 5 - 10mg/kg im, 500µg/kg po twice daily

shock 50mg/kg iv, repeat in 3 - 6 hours

hyoscine

(scopolamine USAN)

antimuscarinic

indications

anaesthetic premed, motion sickness

side effects

Very similar to atropine but more CNS effects. Causes sedation and amnesia in people but these are not obvious in animals.

dose

premed - dog & cat 10 - 20µg/kg sc / im

antiemetic - dogs and cats 30µg/kg four times daily sc

notes

From henbane (Hyoscyamus niger), thorn apple (Datura stramonum) and many other plants - often seen in poisoning cases.
