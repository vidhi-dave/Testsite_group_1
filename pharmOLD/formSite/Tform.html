Information on doses for drugs licensed for use in animals should be obtained from the IVS or the manufacturer’s data sheet. The information here is intended as a guide only. Remember, unlicensed drugs are given on your responsibility. Remember withholding times in food animals too. The list of drugs available in NZ (and their price!) is subject to change. theophylline

phosphodiesterase inhibitor

indications

mild congestive heart failure, some respiratory disease

side effects

restlessness, tachyarrhythmias

dose

Dog 9 mg/kg po 3 - 4 times daily

Cat 4 mg/kg po 2 - 3 times daily

slow release capsules: dog 20mg/kg twice daily

note

therapeutic level in people 10 - 20µg/mL

toxic level dogs > 65µg/mL

thyroid hormones

see levothyroxine and liothyronin

tocainide

class 1 antiarrhythmic

indications

ventricular tachyarrhythmias

pharmacokinetics

Absorption rapid and almost complete absorption, food alters rate but not extent, peak levels at 0.5-2 hours on an empty stomach

Distribution minimal plasma protein binding, Vd in dogs 1.7 l/kg

Metabolism minimal hepatic first pass effect

Elimination predominantely renal with up to 50% unchanged, alkalisation of the urine may decrease the amount excreted but acidification does not increase it

side effects

gut effects, hypotension, keratopathies

contraindications

patients with 2nd or 3rd degree A-V block

care

heart failure

dose

Dog - 10 - 20 mg/kg 3 - 4 times daily with food

tolbutamide

oral hypoglycaemic

indications

non insulin depentent diabetes

side effects

hypoglycaemia, hepatopathy

precautions

care with liver or kidney disease

dose

dog 20 - 100 mg/kg daily po as necessary

tranexamic acid

parenteral coagulant

indications

treatment of overdose with fibrinolytic agents, plasma fibrinogen<= lmg/ml, haematuria, (uncontrollable haemorrhage), oral toxicoses (to induce emesis)

contraindications

pregnancy, intravascular clotting

side effects

rarely multiple intracirculatory thromboses

dose

all species 5 - 25 mg/kg iv,im,sc

triamcinolone

depot glucocorticoid

indications

inflammation

side effects

all the usual corticosteroid side effects

dose

dogs 0.1 - 0.25mg/kg po once daily, same dose sc or im once only

often used intralesionally - 1.2 - 1.8mg at 2 - 3 sites max 6 mg

note

commonly used intermediate acting - duration approximately 7 days after injection

Tris-EDTA solution

ear flushing solution

indications

chronic bacterial / fungal infection of external ear

recipe

12g Tris base

6.05g EDTA disodium

water to 1L

adjust pH to 8 with HCl (about 0.25mL conc HCl / L)

care

ruptured ear drum

dose

fill ear canal, massage and empty; 4 times daily reducing to once weekly
