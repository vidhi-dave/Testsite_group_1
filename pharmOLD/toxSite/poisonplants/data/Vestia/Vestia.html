Vestia

Vestia foetida

Description

Vestia foetida is an erect, evergreen shrub that stands 1.5-2 metres high. Its leaves are simple bright glossy green and elliptical, and emit an unpleasant foetid odour when bruised. The flowers are yellow, up to 25mm long (Plate 38), and the fruit contains numerous orange brown seeds. It flowers in the spring. It is a native of Chile and is a regularly used ornamental garden shrub which has now become naturalised and spread widely in New Zealand.

Similar plants

Other garden Solanaceae

Distribution

Gardens

Toxin

Unknown

Species affected

Strongly suspected poisoning has been reported in goats in New Zealand. The animals were seen eating the plant and some 3 hours later were showing nervous signs.

Clinical signs

The signs included ataxia, particularly when forced to bear weight, muscle twitching of the head and hindquarters, and their breath had a distinct foetid smell consistent with the smell of crushed V.foetida leaves. As the poisoning progressed muscle fasciculations became severe, salivation occurred, heart rate increased and rectal temperature was elevated. Other nervous signs such as head pressing were also noted. One of the two affected animals died.

Post mortem signs

The main pathological features were mild fatty changes in the liver with severe centrilobular necrosis, which was noted on histopathological examination.

References

McKeogh, V.L, Collett, M.G. and Parton, K.H. (2005). Suspected Vestia foetida poisoning in young goats. N Z vet. J. 53. 352-355.
