plant

Glory lily

Gloriosa superba

Other names

Climbing lily, Tiger’s claws, flame lily

Description

Leaves ovate/long lanceolate (12 x 2.5cm) with tendril like extensions of leaves used for climbing. Flowers are linear (up to 7.5cm long) and are yellow at first, turning red later, held horizontally or flexed back over the stalk. Fruit is a capsule, splitting to reveal seeds. Tuberous root.

Similar plants

Distribution

Cultivated (usually greenhouses). Originally from Uganda.

Toxin

Colchicine and gloriosine, 1,2 didemethylcolchicine 2, 2,3 didemethylcolchicine 1, 3 didemethylcolchicine, N formyl N deacetylcolchicine, colchicoside (alkaloids).?The seed coat, tubers, leaves and pericarp are all poisonous. Toxic levels are the highest at the beginning of the growth season and decline after that. Chemical breakdown occurs when the plant dies.

Species affected

All.

Clinical signs acute

May include gastrointestinal irritation, severe and bloody diarrhoea, vomiting, abdominal pain, Vaginal bleeding, numbness of mouth, severe alopecia, dehydration, haemoglobinuria, nervous excitement, unconsciousness, weakness, swelling of limbs.

Clinical signs chronic

Post mortem signs

May include gastrointestinal irritation, severe alopecia, dehydration, haemoglobinuria, and swelling of limbs.

Diagnosis

History of exposure and clinical signs.

Differential diagnosis

Colchicum autumnale poisoning

Treatment

Fluids, monitoring of renal function and blood pressure, treatment for diarrhoea.

Prognosis

Death can occur, otherwise recovery is slow.
