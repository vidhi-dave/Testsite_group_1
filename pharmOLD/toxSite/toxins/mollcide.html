Molluscicides

metaldehyde
methiocarb
iron EDTA

Molluscicides in pelleted, flake or liquid formulations are utilized in situations where snails and slugs are a problem.  Copper sulphate is a popular molluscicide in general agricultural use and is not regarded as a special toxic hazard; arsenic and pentachlorophenol have now been phased out of use.  Metaldehyde and methiocarb are used widely in the domestic and commercial areas of gardening. Iron EDTA (ferric sodium ethylenediamine tetraacetic acid) is an additional molluscicide introduced in the late 1990’s.

The incidence of poisoning is high in dogs and cats as well as wild life including hedgehogs and birds. There are estimates of up to 5000 pet poisonings in New Zealand per year, but the incidence of primary and secondary poisoning to wild life, including earthworms, carabid beetles and other gardener friendly creatures is unknown.
